Hoon Lee is a licensed acupuncturist (L.Ac.) and doctoral candidate in Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (DAOM) at South Baylo University. He holds an undergraduate degree in statistics from Korea University and is the founder and clinic director of CALee Acupuncture in Buena Park, California.

He immigrated to the United States in his thirties and built a clinical practice and independent research program over two decades — working at the intersection of traditional Korean medicine and contemporary evidence-based practice.

His research focuses on the epistemological foundations of Korean medicine: why Korean acupuncture's diagnostic reasoning is structurally distinct from both biomedicine and modern TCM, and what kind of research framework is actually appropriate for evaluating it.

Research Focus

  • Epistemological foundations of Korean acupuncture — why the diagnostic framework requires its own evaluative paradigm
  • Constitutional individuation in clinical practice — Sasang and Eight Constitution Medicine as precision frameworks
  • Integrative medicine research methodology — why standard RCT design structurally misaligns with Korean medicine's clinical logic
  • The convergence between Korean medicine's relational process thinking and contemporary systems biology and network medicine
  • Classical and modern medicine as complementary lenses for reading chronic and geriatric conditions

Clinical Practice

Hoon Lee practices Korean acupuncture at CALee Acupuncture in Buena Park, California, specializing in chronic pain, sciatica, peripheral neuropathy, and complex multi-system conditions. His clinical approach is grounded in constitutional diagnosis — Sasang and Eight Constitution Medicine — and Saam acupuncture, a classical 17th-century Korean needle method rarely practiced outside Korea.
