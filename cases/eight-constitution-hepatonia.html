Eight Constitution Medicine: The Hepatonia (Liver-Strong) Pattern

Step 1 · Presentation

The Presenting Picture

Consider the recurring pattern of the Hepatonia type. Eight Constitution Medicine (ECM) reads each person as having a fixed rank-order of organ-system strengths; the Hepatonia leads with the Liver and is weakest in the Lung. Such individuals often tolerate substantial food well, tend toward a calm and unhurried steadiness, and may feel better in warm, still conditions than in cold, windy, or over-stimulating ones.

Beyond the chief complaint, a cluster of accompanying signs recurs in this pattern:

  • A constitutional ordering led by a strong Liver, weakest in the Lung
  • Often a steady, calm, unhurried disposition
  • A tendency to tolerate and absorb well, sometimes toward retention
  • A characteristic response to specific foods and environments used in ECM assessment

ECM differs from Sasang in method — it emphasizes a fixed eight-fold organ ordering and uses pulse and response to guide constitution-specific treatment and diet — but shares the core premise that the person, not the disease alone, is the unit of diagnosis.

Step 2 · Constitutional Reading

Reading the Pattern: Hepatonia — Liver-Strong (목양체질)

In ECM, the Hepatonia’s Liver-strong / Lung-weak ordering shapes both vulnerability and management. Because the framework treats this ordering as constitutionally fixed, its therapeutic logic is to avoid over-strengthening the already-strong organ and over-draining the weak one, and to use constitution-specific dietary and treatment strategies accordingly.

The Hepatonia overlaps conceptually with the Sasang Taeeumin (both Liver-strong, Lung-weak), and the two frameworks can be read as complementary lenses on a related tendency toward absorption and retention. The library treats them as distinct systems that can illuminate each other rather than as identical.

The Hepatonia leads with a strong Liver and a weak Lung — so its management avoids over-boosting the strong organ, and its diet and treatment are constitution-specific.

Step 3 · Diagnostic Logic

Why This Reading — and Not Another

The distinguishing feature of ECM is the strictness and fixity of the organ ordering, and the correspondingly precise constitution-specific diet and treatment it prescribes. Reading a person as Hepatonia rather than a neighboring type turns on careful assessment; a misread ordering sends the constitution-specific strategy the wrong way — the central pitfall the next case in this series examines.

The practical value is directional: for a Hepatonia, constitution-appropriate strategies that respect the fixed organ ordering form the throughline, distinct from symptomatic treatment alone.

This overview describes a constitutional framework and tendency, not a diagnosis of any individual, and is a teaching lens rather than a prescription; constitutional assessment is individualized, and responses vary by individual.
