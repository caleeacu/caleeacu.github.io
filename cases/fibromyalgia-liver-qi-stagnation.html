Clinical Case Library · Case 008

Widespread Pain (Fibromyalgia) Read as Liver Qi Stagnation

Widespread, migrating body pain with fatigue, poor sleep, and a strong link to stress resists a single structural explanation. A Liver Qi stagnation reading offers a coherent account of why the pain moves, why it tracks emotion, and where treatment is directed.

Pain & Musculoskeletal Pattern: Liver Qi Stagnation (肝氣鬱結) Keyword: fibromyalgia liver qi stagnation acupuncture

Step 1 · Presentation

The Presenting Picture

Consider a composite presentation seen repeatedly in clinic: an adult with diffuse, aching, sometimes migrating pain across multiple body regions, tender points, non-restorative sleep, and marked fatigue. The pain and tension noticeably rise and fall with emotional stress; the person describes feeling wound-up, easily frustrated, with a sense of physical tightness that will not release.

Beyond the chief complaint, a cluster of accompanying signs recurs in this pattern:

  • Widespread, shifting pain and tenderness that does not localize to one joint or structure
  • A strong link between symptom flares and stress, frustration, or emotional strain
  • Non-restorative sleep, fatigue, and a sense of being physically “wound-up”
  • Tightness that eases with relaxation, movement, or emotional release, and worsens when suppressed

Because no single structure explains diffuse migrating pain, a structural model struggles here. The classical reading focuses on the qualities that do pattern the picture: the movement of the pain, its stress-linkage, and the tension — all signatures of disordered Qi flow rather than local tissue damage.

Step 2 · Constitutional Reading

Reading the Pattern: Liver Qi Stagnation (肝氣鬱結)

The Liver, in classical theory, governs the free and smooth flow of Qi (肝主疏泄) and is the organ most sensitive to emotional strain. When that free flow is constrained — Liver Qi stagnation — Qi backs up and moves erratically, producing tension, migrating pain, and a tight, frustrated affect. The stress-linkage and the shifting quality of the pain are the defining signs.

On the four axes this reads as an Interior, functional disorder that is more a matter of disordered movement than of clear Deficiency or Excess at onset — though prolonged stagnation can generate Heat or deplete Blood over time. The key axis reading is the disturbed flow of Qi, which is why the pain migrates rather than fixing.

When pain moves with mood and will not stay put, the target is the flow of Qi itself — the direction is to restore smooth movement, not to treat any one sore point.

Step 3 · Diagnostic Logic

Why This Reading — and Not Another

The differential is against a fixed, structural pain (a specific joint or Bi obstruction) which stays in one place, tracks physical load rather than emotion, and does not migrate. Those are treated locally. The Liver Qi picture is defined precisely by what those lack: movement, stress-linkage, and a whole-body tension pattern.

It also differs from a pure deficiency-fatigue pattern (case 048 territory, Spleen Qi), where the dominant note is emptiness and exhaustion rather than wound-up tension. Here the tension and constraint are primary; treating it as simple depletion would miss the stagnation that needs to move.

For this Liver Qi stagnation reading, the treatment direction is to restore the smooth flow of Qi, release tension, and support the Liver’s regulating function, often alongside attention to sleep and stress. This describes a direction of treatment, not a guaranteed outcome; responses vary by individual.
