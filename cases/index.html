Clinical Case Library

Fifty synthetic teaching cases showing how constitutional diagnosis actually works: what a practitioner sees, how the pattern is read, and why the same symptom can call for different treatment directions in different constitutions.

About These Cases Every case in this library is a representative teaching case, synthesized from recurring clinical patterns. None describes a specific patient, and no identifying information from any real patient is used. These are educational illustrations of diagnostic reasoning — not testimonials, not medical advice, and not claims of treatment outcome. Individual situations vary; consult a licensed healthcare provider for any medical concern.
