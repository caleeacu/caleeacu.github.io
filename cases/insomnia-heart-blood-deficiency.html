Clinical Case Library · Case 046

Insomnia Read as a Heart Blood Deficiency Pattern

Not all insomnia is the same. Waking repeatedly, with light unrefreshing sleep, palpitations, and fatigue, points to Heart Blood deficiency — the Blood that should house the spirit at night is insufficient. A different pattern from the fiery insomnia of case 047.

Step 1 · Presentation

The Presenting Picture

Consider a composite presentation seen repeatedly in clinic: an adult who falls asleep without great difficulty but wakes repeatedly through the night, with light, dream-disturbed, unrefreshing sleep. Alongside this are palpitations or a fluttering awareness of the heartbeat, fatigue, poor memory or concentration, and a pale, depleted quality. The person feels drained rather than wired.

Beyond the chief complaint, a cluster of accompanying signs recurs in this pattern:

  • Waking repeatedly through the night; light, dream-disturbed sleep
  • Palpitations or an awareness of the heartbeat, especially when lying down
  • Fatigue, poor concentration or memory, and a pale, depleted quality
  • Feeling drained rather than agitated or wired

The classical framework treats insomnia not as one condition but as a family of patterns, distinguished by how sleep fails and what accompanies it. The deficiency signature here — waking rather than not falling asleep, palpitations, fatigue, pallor — is what selects this reading.

Step 2 · Constitutional Reading

Reading the Pattern: Heart Blood Deficiency (心血虛)

Classically the Heart houses the spirit (心藏神), and the Blood is what anchors and nourishes it. At night, the spirit should return to and rest in the Blood of the Heart. When Heart Blood is deficient, the spirit lacks its residence and floats — so sleep is light, easily broken, and dream-disturbed, and the under-nourished Heart itself produces palpitations.

On the four axes this reads as Interior, Deficient (Heart Blood), an emptiness pattern without heat. This is precisely the differential developed in the framework paper: the same complaint of insomnia divides into a deficiency pattern (Heart Blood), an excess-heat pattern (Liver Fire, case 047), and a phlegm-heat pattern — each requiring a different direction.

Sleep that breaks repeatedly, with palpitations and fatigue, is read as a spirit without enough Blood to rest in — so the direction is to nourish Blood and settle the spirit, not to drain heat.

Step 3 · Diagnostic Logic

Why This Reading — and Not Another

The decisive contrast is with the Liver Fire insomnia (case 047). There the person cannot fall asleep, is agitated, irritable, red-faced, with vivid dreams and a wired quality — an excess-heat pattern calling for clearing and draining. Here the person falls asleep but cannot stay asleep, and is pale, fatigued, and depleted — a deficiency calling for nourishing.

Applying a draining, heat-clearing approach to this Heart Blood deficiency would further deplete an already empty pattern. This is why the framework insists that the differentiation precede the treatment: the same word, insomnia, names opposite configurations.

For this Heart Blood reading, the treatment direction is to nourish Heart Blood and settle the spirit so it can rest at night. Persistent insomnia should be medically evaluated, and where low mood, anxiety, or distress accompany it, assessment by a qualified mental health professional is appropriate; this describes a direction of treatment, not a guaranteed outcome, and responses vary by individual.
