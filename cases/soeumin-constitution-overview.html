The Soeumin Constitution (少陰人): Cold and Digestion

The Soeumin is the constitution of warmth conserved and digestion easily overwhelmed — modest in fire, careful in nature. This overview describes how the type tends to present, as a lens for constitutional reading rather than a portrait of any one person.

Step 1 · Presentation

The Presenting Picture

Consider the recurring pattern of the Soeumin type across many people in clinic. The Soeumin tends toward a more slender or delicate build, runs cold, has a modest and easily-disturbed appetite, digests best with warm and simple food, and prefers warmth generally. The temperament is often careful, precise, inward, and detail-attentive.

Beyond the chief complaint, a cluster of accompanying signs recurs in this pattern:

  • A slender or delicate build with a tendency to run cold
  • A modest, easily-disturbed digestion; best with warm, simple food
  • Cold hands and feet and a general preference for warmth
  • A careful, precise, inward temperament

The organizing idea is a physiology modest in digestive warmth (Yang), so that the warming, transporting functions are easily overwhelmed and depleted. When healthy, this is care and precision; when out of balance, it tips toward cold and weak digestion.

Step 2 · Constitutional Reading

Reading the Pattern: Soeumin (少陰人)

In Sasang theory the Soeumin is characterized by a strong Kidney system (water, conservation) and a relatively weaker Spleen system (digestive warmth and transformation). This makes the digestive fire constitutionally modest — easily dampened by cold food, stress, or overwork — and the body prone to cold when out of balance.

When the Soeumin falls ill, the patterns cluster around cold and digestive weakness: cold-type pain, weak digestion, fatigue heaviest in the morning, and depletion under stress. The Soeumin sciatica case (001) shows this cold-deficient tendency expressed in a specific pain complaint.

The Soeumin’s digestive fire is modest by constitution — so its health lies in protecting and warming that fire, and its illnesses cluster around cold and weak digestion.

Step 3 · Diagnostic Logic

Why This Reading — and Not Another

Reading a person as Soeumin requires the whole configuration to converge: the cold-prone, slender physiology, the modest and warmth-preferring digestion, the careful inward temperament, and the cold-deficient vulnerabilities. The contrast with the Soyangin is instructive — where the Soeumin runs cold with weak digestion, the Soyangin runs hot with strong digestion; mistaking one for the other reverses the treatment direction.

The practical value is directional: for a Soeumin, protecting and warming the digestive Yang and avoiding cold depletion is the constitutional throughline across complaints.

This overview describes a constitutional tendency, not a diagnosis of any individual, and is a teaching lens rather than a prescription; constitutional assessment is individualized, and responses vary by individual.
