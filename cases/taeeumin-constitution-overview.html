The Taeeumin Constitution (太陰人): Pattern and Tendencies

The Taeeumin is the constitution of absorbing and storing — strong at taking in, weaker at dispersing. This overview describes how the type tends to look, feel, and fall ill, as a lens for constitutional reading rather than a description of any one person.

Step 1 · Presentation

The Presenting Picture

Rather than a single patient, consider the recurring pattern of the Taeeumin type as it presents across many people in clinic. The Taeeumin tends toward a solid, substantial build, gains weight readily, sweats freely (and often feels better for it), and has a strong appetite and capacity to absorb. The temperament is typically steady, patient, and enduring, sometimes slow to move or to change.

Beyond the chief complaint, a cluster of accompanying signs recurs in this pattern:

  • A solid, substantial build with a tendency to gain weight and retain
  • Free sweating that often brings relief, and a strong appetite
  • A steady, patient, enduring temperament; slow to change
  • A tendency to accumulate — weight, fluid, phlegm-damp — when out of balance

The organizing idea is a physiology strong at absorbing and storing but relatively weak at dispersing and circulating. When healthy, this is endurance and substance; when out of balance, it tips toward accumulation.

Step 2 · Constitutional Reading

Reading the Pattern: Taeeumin (太陰人)

In Sasang theory the Taeeumin is characterized by a strong Liver system (absorbing, storing inward) and a relatively weaker Lung system (dispersing, releasing outward). This inward-strong / outward-weak axis explains the type’s substance and endurance, and also its besetting vulnerability: a tendency for things to accumulate rather than move — weight, dampness, phlegm.

When the Taeeumin falls ill, the patterns therefore cluster around accumulation and stagnation: phlegm-damp, weight and metabolic issues, and congestion of the normally free outward flow. Cases elsewhere in this library — the Taeeumin knee (004) and the phlegm-damp metabolic reading (018) — show this tendency expressed in specific complaints.

The Taeeumin is strong at storing and weak at dispersing — so its health lies in keeping things moving, and its illnesses cluster around accumulation.

Step 3 · Diagnostic Logic

Why This Reading — and Not Another

Reading a person as Taeeumin is not done from build alone. The whole configuration must converge: the substantial physiology, the free relieving sweat, the strong absorptive appetite, the steady enduring temperament, and the accumulation-type vulnerabilities. Any one sign is weak evidence; the convergence is the reading.

The practical value is directional: for a Taeeumin, supporting the weaker dispersing function and preventing accumulation is the constitutional throughline, whatever the specific complaint. This shapes the emphasis of treatment across very different presentations in the same person.

This overview describes a constitutional tendency, not a diagnosis of any individual, and is a teaching lens rather than a prescription; constitutional assessment is individualized, and responses vary by individual.
