Core Framework — Three Axes

Relational Process

Illness is not a fixed entity but a conditional expression of imbalance in physiological dynamics. Diagnosis interprets the pattern of that imbalance — not a disease category.

Constitutional Individuation

Sasang and Eight Constitution typologies provide the foundational diagnostic layer. The same biomedical diagnosis may correspond to entirely different constitutional patterns.

Non-Fixed Self

The patient is not a static entity. Health and disease are understood as ongoing processes of self-organization — drawing on Buddhist anātman and process philosophy.

About the Author

Hoon Lee

L.Ac., DAOM(c)

SSRN Profile → Google Scholar → CALee Acupuncture →

Hoon Lee is a licensed acupuncturist and doctoral candidate in Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine at South Baylo University. He holds an undergraduate degree in statistics from Korea University and is the founder of CALee Acupuncture in Buena Park, California.

His research focuses on the epistemological foundations of Korean medicine — arguing that diagnostic reasoning in Korean acupuncture operates through a relational, process-based framework that is structurally distinct from both biomedicine and modern TCM, and that existing research paradigms systematically misalign with how Korean medicine actually works.
